# Countdown Types

- **Standard:** fixed approach toward a known outcome.
- **Dynamic:** advances at an uneven pace based on play.
- **Chase:** tracks pursuit and distance pressure.
- **Long-term:** tracks slow changes across sessions.
- **Progress:** tracks effort toward a task or journey.

## Rule

Each step should represent a visible change in the fiction, not just a hidden clock tick.

## Build Method

1. Name the outcome at 0.
2. Count backward through visible changes.
3. Decide what makes the clock advance.
4. Make sure the PCs can interact with it somehow, even if only indirectly.

## Good Uses

- invasions, coups, and rituals
- travel races
- chases
- reinforcements arriving
- a relationship or political situation deteriorating
- a location collapsing or becoming unusable

## Failure Mode To Avoid

Do not create a countdown that advances only in your notes and never changes the shared fiction. Players should feel the clock even when they do not know the exact numbers.
