# Scene Structure

Every planned scene should answer six questions:

- Why is this scene happening now?
- What does someone want?
- What stands in the way?
- What can change here?
- What information or cost might emerge?
- How can the scene end without stalling the campaign?

## Rule

If the scene does not change anything, cut it or merge it into another scene.

## Strong Openings

Use an opening that immediately signals what kind of scene this is:

- an arrival at a charged place
- visible fallout from something off-screen
- an NPC demand or accusation
- the start of a ritual, assault, chase, or collapse
- a moment of emotional vulnerability before pressure lands

## Exit States

Try to prepare two or three likely ways a scene can end:

- success with new direction
- partial success with cost
- failure that advances danger instead of stopping play
