# Role Mix

Use these role jobs:

- **Standard:** average members of a faction. Use them as the encounter backbone, to hold space, guard fragile allies, or make formations and faction identity visible.
- **Bruiser:** tough close-quarters threat. Expect high damage, lower accuracy, lower Difficulty, and enough durability to occupy melee-focused PCs.
- **Leader:** stronger-than-average enemy whose real power comes from commanding, summoning, or coordinating allies. A Leader without followers is often much less impressive.
- **Support:** a lower-damage enemy that enhances allies, imposes conditions, or changes the field. Supports should matter enough that focusing them down feels tactically meaningful.
- **Ranged:** fragile if pinned down, dangerous when left alone. Use them to punish open ground, force movement, or protect a position.
- **Skulk:** opportunistic ambusher that exploits openings, mobility, or stealth. Use Skulks to threaten the backline or create uncertainty.
- **Horde:** many weak enemies abstracted into one unit. Great for scale, swarm pressure, and scenes where damage output should visibly decline as the group is cut down.
- **Minion:** very easy to kill but dangerous in numbers. Use Minions when you want cinematic sweep and quick resolution rather than long enemy turns.
- **Social:** nonstandard adversary that applies pressure through conversation, status, coercion, or emotional cost instead of straightforward violence.
- **Solo:** centerpiece threat for a whole party. Use Solos when one enemy should dominate the scene, often with extra action economy or major scene-defining features.

## Composition Rule

The encounter should ask more than one tactical question.

## Selection Heuristics

- If the PCs need a wall to break through, start with Standards or Bruisers.
- If the enemy side should feel organized, add a Leader or Support.
- If you want movement to matter, add Ranged, Skulks, or an environment that punishes position.
- If you want scale without bookkeeping, use Hordes or Minions.
- If you want one memorable centerpiece, use a Solo and make sure the rest of the scene supports it.
