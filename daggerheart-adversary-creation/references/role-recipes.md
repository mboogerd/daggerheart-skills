# Role Recipes

Use these as creation defaults:

- **Standard:** average offense and defense for tier. Build one feature that expresses faction style or battlefield identity.
- **Bruiser:** lower or average accuracy, lower Difficulty, strong thresholds and HP, high damage, often area attacks or forced movement.
- **Leader:** higher-than-average stats plus at least one command, rally, summon, or ally-enabling feature. Most effective when other enemies can exploit that feature.
- **Support:** slightly weaker direct numbers, higher Stress budget, low-to-middling damage, and features that heal, protect, hinder, restrain, or alter conditions.
- **Ranged:** fragile if pinned down, strong distance damage, often a reaction or movement trick to avoid melee collapse.
- **Skulk:** mobility, hidden information, repositioning, opportunistic bonuses, or ways to punish isolation.
- **Social:** pressure through stress, leverage, humiliation, reputation, coercion, or negotiations under threat.
- **Horde:** low Difficulty, low attack modifier, average thresholds, high damage that drops sharply after the Horde loses about half its HP.
- **Minion:** 1 HP, 1 Stress, fixed damage, `Minion (X)`, and `Group Attack`; best for cinematic volume and low bookkeeping.
- **Solo:** high durability, high damage, and features that let the enemy remain active enough to matter against an entire party.

## Creation Rule

Start by deciding what choice the adversary forces from the PCs. Build numbers and features to support that choice.

## Numeric Tendencies

- **Bruiser:** high or severe damage, high HP, slightly higher thresholds, lower Difficulty.
- **Leader:** high Difficulty, strong thresholds, good damage, ally-facing features.
- **Support:** average or weak attack, middling HP, higher Stress if their abilities spend it.
- **Standard:** moderate numbers in every category.
- **Horde:** strong opening damage, weaker end-state damage.
- **Minion:** fixed damage and low survivability by design.

## Motive Rule

Write motives as verbs or drives the GM can actually use: arrest, protect, flee, humiliate, consume, worship, study, delay, steal, pursue.

## Lookup Rule

After you pick the role, stop using this file for numbers and load the exact `tier-role` file for that role and tier. This file gives qualitative shape. The `tier-role` files give concrete numeric bands and defaults.
