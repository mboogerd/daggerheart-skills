# Arc Structure

An arc should be short enough to stay reactive and long enough to develop pressure.

## Build Order

1. Pick one main pressure.
2. Tie it to at least one PC belief, bond, history, or need.
3. Choose 3 to 4 beats that materially change the situation.
4. Decide what worsens if the PCs hesitate.
5. Seed the next arc before this one ends.

## Rule

Plan consequences and momentum, not fixed scenes in exact order.

## What A Good Arc Does

- moves at least one major relationship or threat
- gives the PCs chances to succeed, fail, and choose what matters most
- exposes more of the setting than the table knew at the start
- ends with a strong change in circumstance rather than a soft fade-out

## Default Shape For 3 To 4 Sessions

- **Session 1:** introduce the pressure, show what is at stake, and reveal the first complication.
- **Session 2:** widen the field, complicate loyalties, or expose the cost of delay.
- **Session 3:** force a major choice, confrontation, or race against escalation.
- **Session 4 if needed:** deliver the climax, fallout, and the seed of the next arc.

## Arc Questions

Answer these while planning:

- Who benefits if the PCs fail or hesitate?
- What false assumption might the PCs begin with?
- What should be true at the end of the arc that was not true at the start?
- What ongoing thread can survive the arc and keep growing?
