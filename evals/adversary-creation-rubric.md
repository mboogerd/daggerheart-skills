# Adversary Creation Rubric

Grade each adversary output on a 1 to 5 scale.

## Trigger Fit

- Did `daggerheart-adversary-creation` activate for the right kind of prompt?
- Did it avoid taking over scaling, budgeting, or scene-planning work?

## Tier And Role Fit

- Did the output choose the right tier and role?
- If those were omitted, did it default or ask a follow-up correctly?
- Does the resulting adversary actually behave like that role?

## Mechanical Correctness

- Do the numbers fit the matching `tier-role` reference?
- Are role-required mechanics present for Minions, Hordes, and similar special cases?
- Do the features reinforce the battlefield job instead of fighting against it?

## Structural Correctness

- Are all required fields present?
- Is the output easy to parse against the template?
- Are there 1 to 3 usable features?

## Table Usefulness

- Can a GM tell how to run the adversary immediately?
- Is the adversary distinct from a generic enemy of the same role?
- Would the encounter be more interesting because of this stat block?

## Failure Reasons To Track

- wrong role
- wrong tier
- numbers outside safe band
- missing required role mechanics
- overloaded feature list
- unclear battlefield job
- too generic to be worth custom creation
