# Bridge at Willow Ford

## Encounter Premise

- Scene: The PCs must hold a narrow bridge long enough for villagers to finish evacuating carts and children across the river.
- Location: Willow Ford bridge at the edge of a rain-swollen farming village.
- Tier: 1

## Party Assumptions

- Party size: 4
- Notable capabilities: Mixed melee and ranged party with no assumption of flight.
- Desired pressure: Dangerous but survivable defense scene with clear positional stakes.

## Narrative Function

- Story job: Introduce the militia deserters as organized, desperate local antagonists rather than faceless raiders.
- Why this fight happens now: The deserters need the bridge immediately to seize supplies before village reinforcements can arrive.

## Enemy Motives

- Primary objective: Seize the bridge and capture the supply wagons on the far side.
- What makes them stay: Their captain has promised food, coin, and safety to anyone who helps secure the crossing.
- What makes them retreat, surrender, or change tactics: If the bridge is held long enough and their pincer attempt fails, the deserters pull back into the tree line and try to regroup rather than die in place.

## Battle-Point Budget

- Starting budget: 14
- Adjustments: None. This is a baseline fight for four tier 1 PCs.
- Final budget: 14

## Encounter Roster Plan

- Slot: Bridge line militia
  Role: standard
  Points: 2
  Scene job: Hold the center of the bridge and stop PCs from freely repositioning.
  Resolution: create-unnamed
  Notes: These should read as disciplined deserters with shield-and-polearm pressure.
- Slot: Flanking bow militia
  Role: ranged
  Points: 2
  Scene job: Punish PCs who leave cover and force attention away from the bridge mouth.
  Resolution: adapt-existing
  Notes: Adapt from an existing guard or militia-style ranged stat block if available.
- Slot: Deserter sergeant
  Role: leader
  Points: 3
  Scene job: Coordinate the push, rally allies, and signal when the line surges.
  Resolution: create-named
  Notes: The sergeant should only shine while allied militia remain in play.
- Slot: Supply-grab skirmishers
  Role: skulk
  Points: 2
  Scene job: Slip along the riverbank and threaten carts, villagers, or the backline.
  Resolution: lookup-existing-unnamed
  Notes: Prefer an existing bandit or scout-style skulk if one is already in the database.
- Slot: Desperate rabble
  Role: minion
  Points: 1
  Scene job: Create panic and scale without adding much bookkeeping.
  Resolution: lookup-existing-unnamed
  Notes: Use a minion group equal to party size.
- Slot: Wagon-pushers
  Role: support
  Points: 1
  Scene job: Clear space, drag barricades, and make the bridge assault feel coordinated.
  Resolution: create-unnamed
  Notes: This support should buff movement or reposition allies instead of dealing heavy damage.
- Slot: Mud-bank swarm
  Role: horde
  Points: 2
  Scene job: Pressure any PC who drops off the bridge to fight on the slippery riverbank.
  Resolution: create-unnamed
  Notes: This keeps the fallback ground dangerous instead of safe.
- Slot: Panic-starters
  Role: minion
  Points: 1
  Scene job: Slip through gaps, overturn carts, and accelerate the sense of chaos around the evacuation.
  Resolution: create-unnamed
  Notes: Keep this second minion group fictionally distinct from the bridge line rabble.

## Environment Plan

- Environment type: Traversal
- Active pressure: The narrow bridge, slick planks, and flooded banks divide the field and make movement choices matter every round.
- Features or hazards: Slippery footing, carts that can jam the crossing, and a swollen river that threatens anyone driven from the bridge.

## Escalation Plan

- Countdown or trigger: Once three PC attack rolls have been made, the deserter sergeant signals a coordinated surge from both bridge and riverbank.
- What changes when it advances: The skulk and minion pressure arrives at once, forcing the PCs to choose between holding the bridge and protecting the evacuation.

## Victory and Failure States

- Full victory: The villagers and supplies escape, the bridge remains in friendly hands, and the deserters are routed or captured.
- Partial victory: The PCs hold long enough for most villagers to escape, but some supplies are lost or one enemy cell withdraws intact.
- Cost of delay or failure: The deserters seize the crossing, capture supplies, and establish a stronger foothold for later scenes.

## Dependency Handoffs
