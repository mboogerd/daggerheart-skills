# Combat Encounter Planning Rubric

Grade each output on a 1 to 5 scale.

## Activation Fit

- Did the encounter-planning skill activate rather than a narrower neighboring skill?
- Did it stay at the orchestration layer?

## Narrative Grounding

- Does the plan explain what the battle does for the story?
- Are adversary motives and battlefield pressures clear?

## Encounter Structure

- Is the budget plausible and clearly explained?
- Does the role mix create more than one player-facing pressure?
- Is the environment active rather than decorative?

## Composition Quality

- Does the plan clearly mark which roster slots should be looked up, adapted, or created?
- Does it avoid silently reimplementing downstream skills?

## Clarity and Reuse

- Is the output easy to hand off into other skills?
- Could a GM use it as a prep artifact without rewriting it?
