# Frame Structure

Every frame is most useful when introduced in stages rather than dumped all at once.

## Stage 1: Pitch

Present only enough to answer:

- What is happening in the world?
- Why is that exciting?
- What kinds of heroes belong here?

## Stage 2: Foundations

Once the table is interested, add:

- tone and feel
- core themes
- background assumptions
- any distinctions or mechanics players need before making characters

## Stage 3: Character Guidance

Then help players place their characters into the frame:

- which communities, ancestries, and classes fit easily
- which ones need explanation or adaptation
- what kinds of relationships or obligations matter in this world

## Stage 4: Launch

Once the table is aligned:

- build the starting region or map
- use frame-specific session-zero questions
- choose or adapt the inciting incident
- identify the first pressure the PCs cannot ignore

## Custom Frame Rule

When building a custom frame, keep the structure and replace the content. A good custom frame still needs a pitch, tone, themes, character guidance, setting distinctions, a launch incident, and at least one special pressure that makes the world feel different.
