# Frame Fit

Campaign frames are structured starting packages for a campaign. A frame supplies a pitch, a tone, a thematic direction, a starting situation, a collection of player-facing assumptions, and usually some special mechanics or distinctions that make the campaign feel different from baseline fantasy adventure.

## Comparison Axes

Judge frame fit on:

- **Core conflict:** what pressure will drive play forward.
- **Genre shape:** war story, survival fantasy, frontier monster hunting, science-fantasy salvage, and so on.
- **Complexity:** how much extra mechanical or homebrew load the frame adds.
- **Character hook density:** how easy it is for players to imagine characters who belong in the world.
- **Prep burden:** how much custom material the GM will likely need later.

## Frame Hooks

- **The Witherwild:** invasive overgrowth, forest myth, sacred imbalance, and a world where damage to the land is a living threat.
- **Five Banners Burning:** border war, rival powers, espionage, divided loyalties, and the pressure of nations in motion.
- **Beast Feast:** local heroes, monster defense, village ties, strong momentum, and lower overhead for immediate adventurous play.
- **The Age of Umbra:** survival fantasy in a dying world, scarcity of safety, and stories about hope under relentless darkness.
- **Motherboard:** science-fantasy ruins, relic technology, ancient systems, and corruption spreading through magical machines.
- **Colossus of the Drylands:** frontier danger, outlaw energy, giant-monster escalation, and gunslinger-style regional pressure.

## Selection Heuristics

- Choose the frame with the clearest external pressure the table already wants.
- Prefer lower-complexity frames when the group is new to Daggerheart.
- Prefer the frame with the easiest immediate character hooks when the table is undecided.
- If two options are close, pick the one whose inciting incident most naturally launches session one.

## Recommendation Pattern

When recommending a frame, answer:

1. What kind of story it produces.
2. What kind of PCs fit naturally.
3. What kind of prep burden it creates.
4. Why it is a better fit than the nearest alternative.
