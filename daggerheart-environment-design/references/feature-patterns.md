# Environment Feature Patterns

Environment features work like adversary features: passives are always on, actions are used as GM moves, and reactions trigger from specific fictional events.

## Strong Environment Feature Jobs

- make movement dangerous or expensive
- split the group or threaten separation
- expose hidden history or scene texture through consequences
- summon or reveal opportunistic adversaries
- escalate through Fear into a scene-defining change

## Fear Feature Rule

Reserve Fear features for effects that noticeably change the scene:

- a creature emerges
- the space collapses, floods, burns, or shifts
- visibility, footing, or access changes
- an event enters a new phase

## Question Prompts

Good environment features imply questions like:

- What has happened here before?
- What traces of past people or predators remain?
- Who benefits from this place's dangers?
- What new problem appears when the PCs engage with the space?
