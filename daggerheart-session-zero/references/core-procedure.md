# Session Zero Agenda

Use this default structure:

1. Campaign concept and player buy-in.
2. Safety and content boundaries.
3. Logistics.
4. Shared world assumptions.
5. Character creation and party links.
6. One short starter situation if time allows.
7. Wrap-up and feedback.

## Rule

The goal is not to finish the campaign. The goal is to align the table and create enough shared material to prep confidently.

## Detailed Default Timing For A Four-Hour Slot

- 5 to 10 minutes: introductions and pronouns if needed.
- 5 to 10 minutes: explain what session zero will cover and what materials are in use.
- 40 minutes: CATS, safety tools, campaign expectations, and logistics.
- 5 to 10 minutes: break.
- 50 minutes: worldbuilding or frame introduction.
- 5 to 10 minutes: break.
- 70 minutes: character creation and party connections.
- 5 minutes: break.
- 30 minutes optional: a short introductory or non-canon scene.
- 10 minutes: wrap-up, questions, and feedback.

## What Must Be Decided By The End

- Whether the table wants this campaign.
- Tone and content boundaries.
- Play cadence and logistics.
- Enough setting information for characters to belong.
- At least one connection or meaningful tie per character.
- What the GM should prep for session one.
