# CATS

Use CATS when the table needs a simple structure:

- **Concept:** What is this campaign about?
- **Aim:** What kind of play experience do we want?
- **Tone:** How serious, hopeful, dark, comedic, or intense is it?
- **Subject:** What topics are in bounds, out of bounds, or off-screen?

## Prompting Rule

Ask one clean question per category. Do not bury safety questions under lore.

## What To Learn In Each Category

### Concept

Find the big-picture premise. A strong concept usually names a conflict, a type of hero, and a fantasy promise.

### Aim

Find out what the table wants creatively and socially. Some groups want a full character-driven epic. Some want relaxed tactical adventure. Some want to explore a specific theme. This changes how tightly you prep and how hard you push drama.

### Tone

Define the emotional shape of play. Daggerheart can support earnest heroism, danger, comedy, romance, grief, or strangeness, but the table should agree on what is primary.

### Subject

Decide what material is welcome, unwelcome, or off-screen. This includes both content boundaries and topics the table actively wants to explore.

## Follow-Up Topics

After CATS, confirm:

- session length and cadence
- in-person or online format
- how rules lookups and retcons will be handled
- whether the group wants campaign frames, collaborative setting creation, or a GM-led setting
- what kinds of backstory hooks players are excited to see used later
